01 — The Problem
The unmeasured question isn't whether an agent can run long — it's whether it holds the long view: plan far ahead, stay aligned to intent, and get safer rather than drift as it self-improves.
Long-horizon failure is real and measured (METR; Illusion of Diminishing Returns, ICLR 2026) — the failures are execution, not reasoning. The long view breaks three ways: the agent drifts off-plan (OWASP ASI01 Goal Hijack), untrusted terrain hijacks it (ASI02–ASI07), or it self-improves in the wrong direction — reward-hacking and goal drift, as the Darwin Gödel Machine and Anthropic's production-RL study both documented. Today's benchmarks test short episodes or one capability; none measures plan + safety together over a horizon — let alone whether self-improvement makes the agent safer.
02 — The Landscape · why a new benchmark is needed
Every published agent benchmark measures one slice, in one session. The empty quadrant: foresight × safety, sustained over a long horizon, improving (not decaying) as the agent self-improves. That is VISTA Bench.

03 — Direction & Technical Approach
DIRECTION BThe brief's example is one shared agent the team verifies; the real question is broader. So Ranger is a three-model agent system, and the novelty is VISTA, the benchmark. The Scout/Worker split is a published injection defense (Dual-LLM → CaMeL); the Dreamer adds guardrailed recursive self-improvement (Reflexion · Generative-Agents reflection · Voyager). The model is the loop, split in three:
▶ SCOUT
The long view. Maps milestones, holds the route, re-plans under drift, escalates the few high-risk forks.
▶ WORKER
The near terrain. Heavily sandboxed; executes steps with no authority or secrets (contains ASI02/03/05).
▶ DREAMER
Between legs. Guardrailed recursive self-improvement (RSI): consolidate skills, purge poisoned beliefs (ASI06), self-audit vs the OWASP Top-10. Verified signal + traceable lineage so it can't reward-hack (the DGM failure).
SHARED — a map/memory with belief revision the agents hand off through · privilege separation + anti-poison gate · calibrated human steering (counters ASI01 goal-hijack, ASI09 trust-exploitation, ASI10 rogue agents).

05 — Why It's Compelling
Everyone races to make agents smarter for one task. No one can yet say whether a long-running agent stays aligned and safe — or quietly drifts — as it improves itself.
You'd ship a benchmark with teeth tied to the OWASP agentic Top-10, and the three-model agent that wins it — real systems work (planner/executor sandboxing, memory, a guardrailed self-improvement loop, evals, model comparisons). Clean ownership for four: benchmark + scoring, Scout (planner), sandboxed Worker + harness, Dreamer + research/demo.
06 — Hard Parts / Risks
THE BET
Foresight + safety are measurable, and the three-model split holds the long view better than one model. A flat premium means we're wrong — still publishable.
RSI IS DOUBLE-EDGED
Self-improvement can amplify misalignment — the DGM hacked its own reward; Anthropic shows reward-hacking generalizes to sabotage. The Dreamer is the guardrailed exception (verified signal, traceable lineage, capped change); if guardrails fail, VISTA catches the drift — which is the point.
NOVELTY
Ranger is a known pattern (CaMeL; Plan-and-Act; Voyager); the contribution is VISTA. No benchmark scores safety improving across self-improvement cycles — the closest show it decaying.
MEASUREMENT
Rubrics that can't be gamed over a moving horizon — hidden ground truth + state-based checks mapped to OWASP ASI threats.
